# Ad Image Creation Guide

## Image Specifications

### Facebook Feed Ad
- **Dimensions:** 1080 x 1080 px (square) or 1200 x 628 px (landscape)
- **Format:** JPG or PNG
- **Text Limit:** Under 20% of image area (Facebook will limit delivery if more)
- **File Size:** Under 30 MB
- **Safe Zone:** Keep important elements 150px from edges

### Instagram Feed Ad
- **Dimensions:** 1080 x 1080 px (square)
- **Format:** JPG or PNG
- **File Size:** Under 30 MB

### Instagram/Facebook Story Ad
- **Dimensions:** 1080 x 1920 px (9:16 vertical)
- **Format:** JPG, PNG, or MP4 (video)
- **File Size:** Under 30 MB
- **Safe Zone:** Top 250px and bottom 310px may be covered by UI elements

## Stock Photo Sources (Free/Affordable)

### Free:
1. **Unsplash** (unsplash.com)
   - Search: "gluten free food", "pizza", "restaurant", "breakfast"
   - High-quality, commercial use allowed

2. **Pexels** (pexels.com)
   - Similar to Unsplash
   - Good food photography section

3. **Pixabay** (pixabay.com)
   - Large selection
   - Some lower quality, but free

### Paid (But Worth It):
1. **Adobe Stock** (stock.adobe.com)
   - $30-50/month for 10 images
   - Very high quality
   - Search: "gluten free", "celiac safe", "gourmet food"

2. **Shutterstock** (shutterstock.com)
   - Similar pricing to Adobe
   - Huge selection

### Use Your Own Photos!
- Visit featured restaurants and take photos
- Higher authenticity
- Unique to your brand
- Just ask restaurant permission

## Quick Canva Tutorial

### Creating Your First Facebook Ad in Canva:

1. **Sign Up:** Go to canva.com, create free account

2. **Create Design:**
   - Click "Create a design"
   - Search "Facebook Ad"
   - Choose size: 1080 x 1080 px (square)

3. **Choose Template:**
   - Browse templates on left sidebar
   - Filter by "Food & Drink"
   - Pick one that fits your vibe

4. **Customize:**
   - Click any text to edit
   - Replace stock photos: Click photo → Upload your own or search Canva library
   - Change colors: Click element → Change color in toolbar
   - Add elements: Click "Elements" tab → Search "food", "fork", "checkmark"

5. **Add Your Text:**
   - Main headline: Large, bold (48-72pt)
   - Subtext: Medium (24-36pt)
   - Website URL: Smaller (18-24pt)

6. **Check Text Overlay:**
   - Keep text under 20% of image
   - Canva has a "Text" overlay detector (use it!)

7. **Download:**
   - Click "Share" button (top right)
   - Select "Download"
   - Choose PNG format
   - Download!

8. **Create Variations:**
   - Duplicate your design (File → Make a copy)
   - Change headline text
   - Test different photos
   - Create 2-3 versions for A/B testing

## Brand Consistency Checklist

For every ad image, ensure:

- [ ] Text is readable at mobile size (test by viewing at 400px width)
- [ ] Brand colors are consistent (if you establish them)
- [ ] Food looks appetizing (bright, fresh, professionally lit)
- [ ] Text overlay is under 20% (use Facebook's text overlay tool)
- [ ] URL is visible and legible
- [ ] Image matches the ad copy message
- [ ] High resolution (1080px minimum)
- [ ] Saved in RGB color mode (not CMYK)
- [ ] File size under 30MB

## Advanced Tips (for later)

### Carousel Ads:
- Create 3-5 images that tell a story
- Each card features a different restaurant
- Swipe through = higher engagement
- Great for "Top 5 GF Restaurants" format

### Video Ads:
- Even simple slideshow videos outperform static images
- Use Canva Video Editor (free)
- 5-10 second clips of food
- Add text overlays
- Export as MP4

### User-Generated Content:
- Ask followers to share their GF meal photos
- Repost with permission (gives credit)
- More authentic, higher trust
- Great for retargeting campaigns
