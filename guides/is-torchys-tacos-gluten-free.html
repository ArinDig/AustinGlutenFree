🌮 Is Torchy's Tacos Gluten Free?

Everything you need to know about eating gluten-free at Torchy's

Home > Guides > Is Torchy's Gluten Free?

📅 Last Updated: March 20, 2026

✅ Yes, Torchy's Tacos Has Gluten-Free Options

But with important caveats. Torchy's corn tortillas are gluten-free, and many fillings are naturally GF. However, Torchy's is NOT a gluten-free facility—cross-contamination is possible.

Torchy's Tacos—the Austin-born chain famous for creative tacos and irreverent names—is a frequent question from the gluten-free community. The good news: you can eat at Torchy's with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity. The reality: you need to be informed and communicate clearly with staff.

This guide covers everything you need to know: what's safe to order, what to avoid, how to minimize cross-contamination risk, and whether Torchy's is worth the effort.

🌮 What's Gluten-Free at Torchy's?

✅ Safe to Order (Naturally GF)

Corn Tortillas

Torchy's corn tortillas are gluten-free. They're made from 100% corn with no wheat flour. However, they're prepared in the same kitchen as flour tortillas, so cross-contamination is possible during prep.

💡 Pro Tip: Always request fresh gloves when ordering if you have celiac disease.

Grilled Proteins (Safe):

  • Grilled beef fajita
  • Grilled chicken
  • Ground beef
  • Carnitas (pulled pork)
  • Refried beans
  • Black beans

Fresh Toppings (Safe):

  • Lettuce, tomatoes, onions, cilantro
  • Jalapeños (pickled or fresh)
  • Guacamole
  • Pico de gallo
  • Salsa verde
  • Cheese (shredded cheddar, cotija, queso fresco)
  • Sour cream
  • Lime wedges

Sides (Safe):

  • Chips and salsa (verify chips are fried in dedicated fryer—many locations share fryers)
  • Side of guacamole

❌ Avoid These Items (Contain Gluten)

⚠️ DO NOT Order If You Have Celiac

Flour Tortillas: Obviously contain gluten. Only order corn tortillas.

Fried Items: Fried chicken, fried avocado, and fried anything else. These are breaded with wheat flour AND cooked in shared fryers with flour tortilla chips.

Queso: Torchy's queso contains wheat flour as a thickener. This is a common hidden gluten source.

Crispy Taco Shells: Some specialty tacos come with crispy taco shells or tostada bases—these may contain gluten or be fried in shared oil.

Tortilla Strips: The crispy strips on some tacos are fried and cross-contaminated.

Trailer Park Taco: Contains fried chicken (breaded). Not safe.

Democrat Taco: Contains fried poblano. Not safe.

⚠️ Cross-Contamination Risks at Torchy's

Torchy's is not a gluten-free facility. Here are the main cross-contamination risks:

1. Shared Prep Surfaces

Flour tortillas and corn tortillas are assembled on the same prep line. Flour dust can contaminate corn tortillas during prep.

💡 Mitigation: Request fresh gloves and ask staff to wipe down the prep surface.

2. Shared Fryers

Many Torchy's locations fry their tortilla chips in the same oil as breaded fried chicken. This makes chips risky for celiacs.

💡 Mitigation: Ask if chips are fried in a dedicated fryer. If not, skip them.

3. Ingredient Cross-Contact

Toppings like cheese, sour cream, and salsa are scooped with the same utensils for all tacos. If flour tortilla remnants get into shared containers, it's a problem.

💡 Mitigation: Request ingredients from fresh containers if you're highly sensitive.

4. Staff Training Variability

Torchy's staff receive basic training on dietary restrictions, but knowledge varies by location and employee. Don't assume they understand celiac cross-contamination protocols.

💡 Mitigation: Be specific: say "I have celiac disease, please use fresh gloves and wipe the surface."

📋 How to Order at Torchy's with Celiac Disease

Step-by-step ordering protocol:

  1. Speak up immediately: "I have celiac disease and need gluten-free corn tortillas with fresh gloves and a clean surface."
  2. Order simple tacos: Stick to grilled proteins (beef, chicken, pork) with fresh toppings. Avoid fried items and queso.
  3. Verify ingredients: Ask "Does this taco have any fried components or queso?" before ordering specialty tacos.
  4. Watch the prep: If possible, watch your tacos being made. Ensure staff change gloves and use a clean surface.
  5. Skip the chips: Unless they confirm dedicated fryer, skip chips to be safe.
  6. Be polite but firm: Staff are usually accommodating if you're clear and respectful.

🙋 Frequently Asked Questions

Is Torchy's Tacos gluten free?

Torchy's has gluten-free options (corn tortillas, many fillings), but it's not a gluten-free facility. Cross-contamination is possible. People with celiac can eat there safely by ordering carefully and requesting fresh gloves/clean surfaces.

Are Torchy's corn tortillas gluten free?

Yes. Torchy's corn tortillas are made from 100% corn with no wheat. However, they're prepared on the same surfaces as flour tortillas, so request fresh gloves and clean prep areas.

Does Torchy's have a gluten-free menu?

Torchy's doesn't publish a dedicated GF menu, but their website and staff can identify GF items. As a rule: corn tortillas, grilled proteins, and fresh toppings are safe. Fried items, queso, and flour tortillas are not.

What should I avoid at Torchy's if I'm gluten free?

Avoid: flour tortillas, all fried items (fried chicken, fried avocado, crispy shells), queso, and crispy tortilla strips. These either contain gluten or are cross-contaminated in shared fryers.

Is Torchy's queso gluten free?

No. Torchy's queso contains wheat flour as a thickener. This is a common hidden gluten source in Tex-Mex queso. Skip it entirely if you're gluten-free.

Can I eat Torchy's chips if I have celiac?

Probably not. Most Torchy's locations fry their chips in the same oil as breaded fried chicken, making them unsafe for celiacs. Ask your specific location if they have a dedicated chip fryer—but many don't.

Is Torchy's safe for celiac disease?

Torchy's can be safe for celiac with proper precautions: inform staff, request fresh gloves, order simple grilled tacos on corn tortillas. However, there's always inherent risk in shared kitchens. If you're highly sensitive or want zero stress, choose a 100% GF restaurant instead.

Which Torchy's tacos are gluten free?

The safest GF options: Beef Fajita, Green Chile Pork, Brushfire (jerk chicken), Mr. Orange (grilled chicken), and Tipsy Chick. Avoid any tacos with fried components (Trailer Park, Democrat, etc.).

Does Torchy's use dedicated gluten-free prep areas?

No. Torchy's uses the same prep line for all tacos. You must request fresh gloves and ask staff to wipe down the surface. They're usually accommodating, but it's not automatic.

Are Torchy's locations consistent with gluten-free protocols?

Quality varies by location and staff training. Some locations are excellent with dietary restrictions; others are less knowledgeable. Downtown Austin and South Lamar locations tend to have more experienced staff with GF requests.
