🌮 Is Chuy's Gluten Free?

Everything you need to know about eating gluten-free at Chuy's Tex-Mex

Home > Guides > Is Chuy's Gluten Free?

📅 Last Updated: March 20, 2026

✅ Yes, Chuy's Has Good Gluten-Free Options

Chuy's offers corn tortillas, enchiladas, tacos, fajitas, and that legendary creamy jalapeño dip (it's GF!). But there's cross-contamination risk on shared griddles.

Chuy's—the Austin-born Tex-Mex chain famous for its creamy jalapeño sauce, fish tacos, and quirky Elvis-shrine decor—is a staple for locals and tourists alike. The good news: Chuy's has solid gluten-free options. The reality: you need to know what to order and communicate about cross-contamination.

This guide covers Chuy's entire gluten-free menu, what's safe, what to avoid, and how to order with celiac disease.

🌮 What's Gluten-Free at Chuy's?

✅ Safe to Order (Naturally GF or Available GF)

🌟 Creamy Jalapeño Dip (MUST-TRY!)

Chuy's most famous item is gluten-free! This legendary dip is served with every meal and beloved by Austinites. It's tangy, creamy, and mildly spicy. You can buy jars to take home, too.

💡 Insider tip: Ask for extra—it's free and you'll want to pour it on everything.

Tortillas:

  • Corn tortillas (soft) — Gluten-free, used for enchiladas and tacos
  • Blue corn tortillas — Also GF, available for some dishes
  • ❌ Flour tortillas — Contain gluten, avoid
  • ❌ Crispy taco shells — Risk of shared fryer, verify before ordering

Proteins (Naturally GF):

  • Grilled chicken (unmarinated)
  • Seasoned ground beef
  • Roasted chicken
  • Grilled shrimp
  • Grilled fish

Enchiladas (on Corn Tortillas):

  • Deluxe Chicken Enchiladas
  • Veggie Enchiladas
  • Chicka-Chicka Boom-Boom Enchiladas (specify corn tortillas)
  • Chile Rellenos with enchiladas (grilled rellenos, not fried)

Tacos (on Corn Tortillas):

  • Baja Fish Tacos (grilled fish—verify not fried)
  • Baja Shrimp Tacos (grilled shrimp)
  • Soft tacos with any grilled protein

Fajitas:

  • Chicken, beef, or shrimp fajitas (skip flour tortillas, use corn)
  • Grilled peppers and onions are naturally GF
  • Request corn tortillas on the side

Salads:

  • Mexi-Cobb Salad
  • Grilled Chicken Salad
  • Taco Salad (skip the fried shell, get in a bowl)
  • Large Dinner Salad

Sides (Mostly Safe):

  • Refried beans (verify—some locations add flour)
  • Black beans
  • Mexican rice
  • Charro beans (pinto beans in broth)
  • Guacamole
  • Pico de gallo

Gluten-Free Sauces:

  • ✅ Creamy Jalapeño (legendary!)
  • ✅ Salsa Fresca
  • ✅ Boom-Boom Sauce
  • ✅ Deluxe Tomatillo
  • ✅ Hatch Green Chile
  • ✅ Chipotle Sauce
  • ✅ Ranchero Sauce

❌ Avoid These Items (Contain Gluten or High Cross-Contamination Risk)

⚠️ DO NOT Order If You Have Celiac

Flour Tortillas: Obviously contain gluten. Only order corn tortillas.

Fried Items: Chicken flautas, crispy tacos, tostadas, chips (if fried in shared oil). High cross-contamination risk in shared fryers.

Tortilla Soup: May contain flour as thickener—verify with kitchen.

Queso: Many Tex-Mex quesos contain flour as thickener. Ask your specific location.

Chalupas: Fried shells, not safe.

Burritos (flour tortilla): Not GF unless on corn tortillas (ask if available).

⚠️ Cross-Contamination Risks at Chuy's

Chuy's official statement: "All our items are prepared where traces of gluten can be found." Here are the main risks:

1. Shared Comal (Griddle)

Corn tortillas and flour tortillas are cooked on the same griddle. Gluten residue from flour tortillas can transfer to corn tortillas during cooking.

💡 Mitigation: Ask if they can cook your corn tortillas on a clean section of the griddle. Some locations are accommodating; others may not be able to during busy times.

2. Shared Fryers

Chips, fried items, and other menu components share fryer oil. This makes chips risky for celiacs unless your location has a dedicated chip fryer (rare).

💡 Mitigation: Ask if chips are fried in a dedicated fryer. If not, skip them.

3. Flour in Unexpected Places

Some locations add flour to refried beans as a thickener. Sauces may have flour added. Always verify ingredients with your server or manager.

💡 Mitigation: Ask specifically: "Do your refried beans contain any flour? What about the sauces?"

4. Staff Training Variability

Chuy's acknowledges cross-contamination and recommends talking to the manager about specific needs. Staff training varies by location—some are excellent, others less knowledgeable.

💡 Mitigation: Ask to speak with a manager if you have celiac disease. They can guide you through the safest options at that specific location.

📋 How to Order at Chuy's with Celiac Disease

Step-by-step ordering protocol:

  1. Speak with the manager: "I have celiac disease. Can you help me order safely?"
  2. Request clean prep: Ask for corn tortillas cooked on a clean section of the griddle, fresh gloves, and clean utensils.
  3. Stick to simple dishes: Enchiladas or fajitas with grilled protein on corn tortillas are your safest bets.
  4. Verify sauce ingredients: Ask if refried beans, queso, or sauces contain flour.
  5. Skip the chips (probably): Unless they confirm a dedicated fryer, chips are risky.
  6. Enjoy that creamy jalapeño: It's GF and legendary—dip everything in it!

🌮 Best Gluten-Free Items to Order at Chuy's

What it is: Roasted chicken enchiladas with Boom-Boom sauce, sour cream, cheese

Why it's safe: Corn tortillas, naturally GF protein and sauce

2. Baja Fish Tacos

Why it's safe: All naturally GF components

💡 Order tip: Verify the fish is GRILLED, not fried (some Chuy's locations fry). "Grilled fish on corn tortillas, please."

3. Fajitas (Chicken, Beef, or Shrimp)

What it is: Sizzling grilled meat with peppers, onions, sides

Why it's safe: All naturally GF, just skip the flour tortillas

💡 Order tip: "Fajitas with corn tortillas only. Please don't bring flour tortillas to the table."

4. Salads (Taco Salad or Mexi-Cobb)

What it is: Big salads with grilled protein, veggies, cheese

Why it's safe: No tortillas involved, naturally GF

💡 Order tip: "Taco salad in a bowl—no fried shell. Extra creamy jalapeño please!"

🙋 Frequently Asked Questions

Is Chuy's gluten free?

Chuy's has many gluten-free options including corn tortillas, enchiladas, tacos, fajitas, salads, and their famous creamy jalapeño sauce. However, Chuy's is NOT a gluten-free facility—cross-contamination is possible on shared griddles and fryers.

Are Chuy's corn tortillas gluten free?

Yes. Chuy's corn tortillas are gluten-free. However, they're cooked on the same griddle as flour tortillas, so cross-contamination is possible. Inform staff about celiac and request clean prep.

Is Chuy's creamy jalapeño sauce gluten free?

YES! The legendary creamy jalapeño dip is gluten-free and Chuy's most iconic item. Pour it on everything—it's included free with your meal. Locals buy jars to take home.

Does Chuy's have a gluten-free menu?

Chuy's doesn't publish a dedicated GF menu, but staff can identify GF items. The general rule: corn tortillas, grilled proteins, and most sauces are safe. Fried items and flour tortillas are not.

Are Chuy's chips gluten free?

It depends. If your location fries chips in a dedicated fryer, they're safe. Most locations share fryers with fried items (flautas, crispy tacos), making chips risky for celiacs. Always ask.

Can I eat at Chuy's with celiac disease?

Yes, with caution. Chuy's CAN accommodate celiac disease if you communicate clearly with staff and stick to simple corn tortilla dishes with grilled proteins. However, cross-contamination is acknowledged by the company—ask to speak with a manager to ensure proper preparation.

Is Chuy's queso gluten free?

Ask your location. Many Tex-Mex quesos contain wheat flour as a thickener. Chuy's menu doesn't clearly state if theirs is GF—verify with kitchen before ordering.

What are the safest gluten-free items at Chuy's?

The safest options: Enchiladas on corn tortillas (Chicka-Chicka Boom-Boom, Veggie, Chicken), Baja tacos with grilled fish on corn tortillas, fajitas with corn tortillas, and salads (skip fried shells).

  • Staff seem unsure or dismissive about your dietary needs

💡 Final Verdict: Should You Eat at Chuy's?

For mild gluten sensitivity: ✅ Yes, Chuy's is fine. Order enchiladas or tacos on corn tortillas and enjoy.

For celiac disease: ⚠️ Proceed with caution. Chuy's CAN accommodate you if you speak with a manager and they take proper precautions. However, the company acknowledges cross-contamination risk.

Our take: Chuy's is decent for casual Tex-Mex when you're informed about what to order. The creamy jalapeño alone is worth visiting once. But if you want zero-stress Mexican food, ATX Cocina or Nômadé are vastly superior for celiacs—order anything on the menu without worry.
