🍗 Is Chick-fil-A Gluten Free?

Everything you need to know about eating gluten-free at Chick-fil-A

Home > Guides > Is Chick-fil-A Gluten Free?

📅 Last Updated: March 20, 2026

✅ Yes! Chick-fil-A Has GF Buns

Chick-fil-A offers a certified gluten-free bun (rare for fast food!) plus grilled nuggets, hash browns, and salads. One of the better fast-food GF options.

Chick-fil-A is surprisingly gluten-free friendly for a fast-food chain. They offer a certified gluten-free bun made from ancient grains (sorghum, amaranth), grilled chicken options, and hash browns cooked in dedicated oil. While not a gluten-free facility (cross-contamination risk exists), Chick-fil-A makes a genuine effort to accommodate celiac diners.

🍗 What's Gluten-Free at Chick-fil-A?

✅ Safe to Order

🍞 Gluten-Free Bun (Certified!)

Chick-fil-A offers a certified gluten-free bun made from sorghum, amaranth, and other ancient grains. It arrives pre-packaged to prevent cross-contact ($1.70 upcharge).

⚠️ The catch: Once opened in the kitchen, Chick-fil-A considers it "no longer gluten-free" due to potential cross-contamination. They recommend you assemble your sandwich yourself for maximum safety.

Breakfast (Good GF Options):

  • ✅ Hash Browns — Cooked in separate oil from breaded items (celiac-safe!)
  • ✅ Grilled Chicken Breakfast — No breading
  • ✅ Bacon, Sausage Patties — Naturally GF
  • ✅ Scrambled Eggs
  • ✅ Hash Brown Scramble Bowls — With grilled chicken or sausage
  • ❌ Breaded Chicken Biscuit/Breakfast items — Contain gluten

Lunch/Dinner:

  • ✅ Grilled Chicken Sandwich — On GF bun ($1.70 extra)
  • ✅ Grilled Nuggets — Not breaded, celiac-safe
  • ✅ Salads — Cobb, Market, Spicy Southwest (skip crispy toppings)
  • ❌ Regular Chicken Sandwich — Breaded with wheat
  • ❌ Regular Nuggets — Breaded, not GF
  • ❌ Waffle Fries — Contain wheat flour in the coating (NOT GF!)

Sides & Snacks (Limited):

  • ✅ Waffle Potato Chips — GF snack in sealed bag
  • ✅ Buddy Fruits Apple Sauce — Kids' side, sealed & GF
  • ✅ Fruit Cup
  • ❌ Waffle Fries — Contain wheat flour in coating

❌ NOT Gluten-Free

⚠️ Major Disappointment: Waffle Fries

Chick-fil-A's famous waffle fries are NOT gluten-free. They contain wheat flour in the coating. This is a major letdown because fries are usually a safe fallback at fast food.

You're stuck with potato chips (sealed bag) or fruit cup as side options.

  • ❌ All breaded chicken (sandwiches, regular nuggets, strips)
  • ❌ Waffle fries (contain wheat flour)
  • ❌ Biscuits
  • ❌ Cookies, brownies
  • ❌ Mac & cheese

⚠️ The Gluten-Free Bun Paradox

Here's the weird thing about Chick-fil-A's GF bun:

🤔 "Gluten-Free" Bun That's... Not Gluten-Free?

Chick-fil-A's official position: The bun is certified gluten-free while packaged. However, once opened in their kitchen, they can no longer guarantee it's gluten-free due to shared surfaces and potential cross-contact.

Their solution: They recommend you assemble your sandwich yourself to minimize cross-contamination.

What this means: Staff will give you the sealed bun, grilled chicken, and toppings separately. You build your sandwich at your table.

Is this annoying? A bit. But it's better than no GF bun at all, and it shows Chick-fil-A takes cross-contamination seriously.

⚠️ Cross-Contamination Risks

Chick-fil-A openly states: "We cannot be certain that any of our foods are completely allergen-free."

Main Risks:

1. Shared Kitchen Surfaces: Breaded chicken (gluten) is prepared alongside grilled chicken (GF) in the same kitchen.

💡 Mitigation: Request glove changes and ask for grilled chicken from a fresh batch.

2. Shared Fryers: While hash browns are cooked in separate oil (good!), waffle fries contain gluten anyway (bad!).

3. Bun Assembly: If staff assemble your sandwich, the GF bun touches surfaces that just touched regular buns.

💡 Mitigation: Assemble your own sandwich (Chick-fil-A's official recommendation).

📋 How to Order at Chick-fil-A with Celiac

Safest ordering approach:

  1. Tell them you have celiac: "I have celiac disease. I'd like a grilled chicken sandwich on the gluten-free bun."
  2. Request glove changes: "Can you please use fresh gloves?"
  3. Ask for self-assembly: "I'd like the bun, chicken, and toppings packaged separately so I can assemble it myself."
  4. Order safe sides: Hash browns (breakfast) or waffle potato chips (lunch)
  5. Skip waffle fries: They contain wheat flour—not GF!

🍗 Best Gluten-Free Orders at Chick-fil-A

1. Grilled Chicken Sandwich on GF Bun

What to order: "Grilled chicken sandwich with gluten-free bun, packaged separately please."

Add toppings: Lettuce, tomato, pickles (all safe)

Side: Hash browns (breakfast) or chips (lunch)

💡 Why it's good: You get an actual sandwich with a real bun. Rare for fast food!

2. Grilled Nuggets

What to order: Grilled nuggets (8-count or 12-count)

Sauce: Most sauces are GF (verify with staff—avoid anything labeled "crispy")

Side: Fruit cup or chips

💡 Why it's good: Simple, safe, protein-packed. Great for kids too.

3. Cobb Salad with Grilled Chicken

What to order: Cobb salad with grilled nuggets

Skip: Crispy bell peppers or any crunchy toppings (breaded)

💡 Why it's good: Big, filling salad with bacon, egg, avocado, cheese. Lower carb option.

4. Hash Brown Scramble Bowl (Breakfast)

What to order: Hash Brown Scramble Bowl with grilled chicken or sausage

💡 Why it's good: Hearty breakfast with GF hash browns, eggs, cheese. Satisfying and safe.

🙋 Frequently Asked Questions

Does Chick-fil-A have gluten-free buns?

Yes! Chick-fil-A offers a certified gluten-free bun made from sorghum and amaranth ($1.70 extra). It comes pre-packaged. Chick-fil-A recommends you assemble your sandwich yourself to minimize cross-contamination once the package is opened.

Are Chick-fil-A grilled nuggets gluten free?

Yes! Grilled nuggets are gluten-free (not breaded). They're one of the safest items for celiacs at Chick-fil-A.

Are Chick-fil-A waffle fries gluten free?

No. This is shocking but true—Chick-fil-A's famous waffle fries contain wheat flour in the coating. They are NOT gluten-free.

Are Chick-fil-A hash browns gluten free?

Yes! Hash browns are gluten-free and cooked in separate oil from breaded items. They're safe for celiacs and one of the best GF breakfast sides.

Is Chick-fil-A safe for celiac disease?

Yes, with precautions. Chick-fil-A offers a certified GF bun and several GF items, but they cannot guarantee completely gluten-free preparation due to shared kitchens. Request glove changes and self-assemble sandwiches for maximum safety.

How much does the gluten-free bun cost at Chick-fil-A?

The gluten-free bun costs $1.70 extra compared to the regular bun.

What sauces are gluten free at Chick-fil-A?

Most Chick-fil-A sauces are gluten-free, but always verify with staff as ingredients can change. Generally safe: Chick-fil-A Sauce, Polynesian, BBQ, Buffalo, Ranch. Check the allergen menu for current info.

💡 Final Verdict

✅ Chick-fil-A is One of the Better Fast-Food GF Options

For celiac disease: ✅ Good option with proper precautions. The certified GF bun is a big deal, and grilled nuggets/hash browns are safe choices.

For mild gluten sensitivity: ✅ Very good. Multiple safe menu items.

Our take: Chick-fil-A makes a real effort to accommodate gluten-free diners, which is rare for fast food. The GF bun (even with self-assembly) is better than bunless burgers elsewhere. The lack of GF waffle fries is disappointing, but hash browns (breakfast) make up for it. Solid fast-food choice for celiacs on the go.
