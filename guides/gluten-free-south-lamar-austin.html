🗺️ Why South Lamar?

South Lamar (affectionately called "SoLa") is Austin's most restaurant-dense corridor, stretching from Lady Bird Lake south to Ben White Blvd. This 3-mile stretch packs 100+ restaurants—and an incredible concentration of gluten-free options.

Perfect for: Food crawls, date nights, visiting Austin for a weekend, or locals who want walkable dining variety.

If you only explore one Austin neighborhood for gluten-free dining, make it South Lamar. This corridor has the city's highest concentration of celiac-friendly restaurants, from 100% dedicated GF spots to James Beard Award winners.

This guide covers 15+ gluten-free restaurants on South Lamar, organized by cuisine type. Whether you're craving pizza, sushi, BBQ, or health-focused bowls, you'll find it here—all within a few miles.

🏆 100% Gluten-Free Restaurants (Zero Stress)

These South Lamar restaurants are entirely gluten-free—order anything on the menu with complete confidence.

Picnik — South Lamar Location

📍 3900 S Lamar Blvd

☕ Cuisine: Healthy Bowls, Coffee & Pastries • 100% GF

💰 Price: $$ (bowls $10-14)

🌾 Why It's Great: The South Lamar location is Picnik's flagship with the full menu: power bowls, breakfast burritos, grain-free baked goods, and excellent coffee. Everything is 100% gluten-free and paleo-friendly. Popular with health-conscious South Austin locals.

💡 Local Tip: The Superhero Bowl (chicken, sweet potato, greens) is the signature. Grab an almond butter cookie for the road. Weekend brunch lines can be long—go mid-morning or order ahead.

Zucchini Kill Bakery

📍 2128 S Lamar Blvd

🧁 Cuisine: Bakery & Sweets • 100% GF

💰 Price: $ (items $3-6)

🍕 Pizza on South Lamar (Gluten-Free Crust)

Pinthouse Pizza — South Lamar

📍 4729 S Lamar Blvd

🍕 Cuisine: Pizza & Craft Beer

💰 Price: $$ (GF pizzas $14-18)

🍣 Upscale Dining (Special Occasions)

South Lamar is home to some of Austin's most celebrated restaurants—several are remarkably GF-friendly.

Uchi

📍 801 S Lamar Blvd

🍣 Cuisine: Japanese (Sushi & Contemporary)

💰 Price: $$$$ ($60-100+ per person)

🌾 GF Options: Most sashimi and many hot dishes are naturally gluten-free. Request GF soy sauce (tamari). The staff are exceptionally knowledgeable—let them guide your omakase with celiac needs noted. This is Austin's most celebrated restaurant (James Beard Award winner).

💡 Local Tip: Book reservations 2-4 weeks ahead. Sit at the bar for the best experience. Tell your server about celiac when you arrive—they'll mark your order and use dedicated prep.

🌮 Mexican Food on South Lamar

Torchy's Tacos

📍 1822 S Lamar Blvd

🌮 Cuisine: Tacos (Austin Chain)

💰 Price: $ ($4-5 per taco)

🌾 GF Options: Corn tortillas are gluten-free (verify with staff about cross-contamination protocol). Most taco fillings are naturally GF—avoid crispy items and queso (contains flour). Popular with locals but understand cross-contamination risk.

💡 Local Tip: The Trailer Park taco (fried chicken) is NOT safe for celiacs (fried). Stick to grilled meats. Request fresh gloves if you have celiac. Expect weekend waits.

Chuy's

📍 1728 Barton Springs Rd (near South Lamar)

🌮 Cuisine: Tex-Mex

💰 Price: $$ ($12-18 per entree)

🌾 GF Options: Corn tortillas are GF. Many entrees can be made GF (fajitas, enchiladas with corn tortillas, taco salads without shell). Staff are familiar with GF requests. Fun, casual atmosphere.

💡 Local Tip: The creamy jalapeño dip is legendary (and GF). Go for weekday lunch to avoid crowds.

🚗 Getting Around South Lamar

Driving: South Lamar is one long road (easy to navigate). Most restaurants have parking lots or street parking. Weekend parking can be tight near popular spots.

Public Transit: Capital Metro Bus Route 801 runs the length of South Lamar with frequent service.

Walking: Individual restaurant clusters are walkable, but the full 3-mile corridor is best driven or rideshared.

Biking: Bike lanes on South Lamar, though traffic is heavy. Many restaurants have bike racks.

💡 South Lamar Dining Tips

✓ Best Time to Visit: Weekday lunch or early dinner (5-6pm) avoids peak crowds. Weekend brunch spots (Bird Bird, Bouldin Creek) have 30-60 minute waits.

✓ Parking Strategy: Most restaurants have small lots. Arrive early or use street parking on side streets. Lamar Union has a parking garage.

✓ Local Secret: South Lamar has the highest concentration of 100% GF restaurants in Austin (Picnik, Zucchini Kill). You can eat your way down the corridor with zero stress.

✓ Weather Consideration: Many South Lamar restaurants have great patios (Odd Duck, Pinthouse, Postino). Spring and fall are perfect. Summer (June-Aug) can be brutally hot.

✓ Combine with Activities: South Lamar is near Zilker Park, Barton Springs Pool, and Lady Bird Lake. Plan meals around outdoor activities.

🙋 Frequently Asked Questions

What's the best 100% gluten-free restaurant on South Lamar?

Picnik for healthy bowls and coffee, or Zucchini Kill Bakery for pastries and treats. Both are entirely gluten-free with zero cross-contamination risk.

Where's the best gluten-free pizza on South Lamar?

Pinthouse Pizza is your only dedicated pizza option directly on South Lamar with GF crust. For better GF pizza, try Via 313 or Home Slice (both have GF crust and are nearby).

What's the best gluten-free restaurant for a special occasion on South Lamar?

Uchi for world-class Japanese (book weeks ahead) or Odd Duck for creative farm-to-table. Both are upscale with excellent GF accommodations.

Are South Lamar restaurants more expensive?

It's a mix. You have budget options ($10-15: Torchy's, Flower Child, Vinaigrette) and splurge spots ($50-100+: Uchi, Odd Duck). More variety than most Austin neighborhoods.

Is South Lamar walkable for a food crawl?

Individual clusters are walkable (e.g., Lamar Union area with Flower Child + Torchy's + Zucchini Kill). The full 3-mile corridor is best driven, biked, or done via rideshare between spots. Plan 2-3 restaurants per outing rather than trying to hit them all in one day.

What's the best gluten-free breakfast on South Lamar?

Bird Bird Biscuit hands down. Dedicated GF kitchen, exceptional GF biscuits and pastries. Arrive early or order ahead—it's wildly popular.
