🗺️ What Counts as "Downtown Austin"?

For this guide, downtown includes: 6th Street (entertainment district), 2nd Street District (shopping/dining), Congress Avenue (Capitol to Lady Bird Lake), Rainey Street (bar district), and the Austin Convention Center area.

Perfect for: Business travelers, convention attendees, tourists, locals working downtown, date nights, pre-show dinners.

Downtown Austin is the city's business and tourist hub—home to the State Capitol, Convention Center, hotels, and the famous 6th Street entertainment district. If you're staying downtown or working in the area, you'll be relieved to know: downtown has excellent gluten-free options within walking distance of major landmarks.

This guide covers 12+ gluten-free restaurants in downtown Austin, organized by what you need: quick business lunches, upscale dinners, grab-and-go, and late-night eats.

🏆 100% Gluten-Free Downtown (Zero Stress)

ATX Cocina — 2nd Street District

📍 218 W 2nd St

🌮 Cuisine: Modern Mexican • 100% GF & Seed Oil Free

💰 Price: $$-$$$ ($15-25 per entree)

🌾 Why It's Perfect: This is THE downtown gluten-free destination. Everything—handmade tortillas, tableside guac, tacos, enchiladas, desserts, full bar—is 100% gluten-free. Located in the heart of 2nd Street District, it's walking distance from hotels, Convention Center, and major office buildings. Popular with business travelers and date nights.

💡 Downtown Tip: Make reservations for dinner (fills up fast). Lunch is easier to walk in. The patio is gorgeous in spring/fall. Perfect for client meals or impressing out-of-town visitors.

💼 Quick Business Lunches (30-45 Minutes)

Need to eat and get back to work? These downtown spots deliver safe GF options fast:

Flower Child — 2nd Street

📍 208 W 2nd St

🥗 Cuisine: Healthy Bowls & Wraps

💰 Price: $$ ($11-15 per bowl)

🌾 GF Options: Most menu items are naturally GF or easily modified. The Mother Earth bowl and Flying Avocado wrap (on GF wrap) are popular. Quick-casual service—order at counter, grab a table. 15-20 minute typical turnaround.

💡 Business Tip: Right next to ATX Cocina on 2nd Street. Order online for faster pickup during lunch rush (12-1:30pm gets packed).

Chipotle — Multiple Downtown Locations

📍 Congress Ave, 6th Street, others

🌮 Cuisine: Fast-Casual Mexican

💰 Price: $ ($8-12 per bowl)

🌾 GF Options: Most items naturally GF (skip flour tortillas, request fresh gloves). Burrito bowls with any protein, all salsas, guac. Reliable nationwide GF protocol. In and out in 10 minutes.

💡 Business Tip: Order on app for pickup—skip the line entirely. Perfect for grabbing lunch between meetings.

Picnik — Congress Avenue

📍 1400 S Congress Ave

☕ Cuisine: Healthy Bowls & Coffee • 100% GF

💰 Price: $$ ($10-14 per bowl)

🌾 GF Options: Everything! 100% gluten-free menu with power bowls, breakfast items, coffee, and pastries. Quick counter service. Walking distance from Capitol.

💡 Business Tip: Slightly south of central downtown but worth the 10-minute walk from Capitol. Great coffee for afternoon meetings.

Thundercloud Subs

📍 Multiple downtown locations (Congress, near Capitol)

🥪 Cuisine: Sandwiches

💰 Price: $ ($8-11 per sub)

🌾 GF Options: Gluten-free bread available for any sub. The California Club and Italian are popular. Fast counter service, good for quick lunches. Austin-based local chain.

💡 Business Tip: Order online for pickup to save time. The Capitol location (on Congress) is most convenient for State government workers.

🌙 Late-Night & 6th Street Options

Heading out on 6th Street for live music? Here's the reality: most 6th Street bars have limited or no GF options. The bar food scene is heavily fried and glutenous.

💡 6th Street Strategy

Eat dinner BEFORE going to 6th Street. Have a proper meal at ATX Cocina, Red Ash, or another downtown GF spot, THEN head to bars for drinks and music.

Late-night options near 6th Street:

  • Torchy's Tacos (East 6th St): Open late, corn tortillas are GF. See our Torchy's GF Guide.
  • Kerbey Lane Cafe: 24-hour diner with GF buns. Nearest location is on Lamar (short Uber from downtown).

🚗 Getting Around Downtown Austin

Walking: Downtown Austin is compact and walkable. Most restaurants in this guide are within 5-15 minute walks of each other.

Public Transit: Capital Metro buses and MetroRail serve downtown. Not essential since most areas are walkable.

Parking: Downtown parking can be expensive ($10-20/day). Use parking apps like ParkWhiz or SpotHero. Street parking has 2-hour limits. Consider Uber/Lyft instead.

Scooters: Lime and Bird scooters are everywhere downtown. Great for quick trips between areas.

💡 Downtown Gluten-Free Dining Tips

✓ Make Reservations: ATX Cocina and Red Ash fill up for dinner, especially weekends. Book 1-2 days ahead minimum.

✓ Use Mobile Ordering: Flower Child, Chipotle, Snap Kitchen, and Picnik all have apps—order ahead to skip lunch lines.

✓ Eat Before 6th Street: Don't rely on finding good GF food on 6th Street at night. Have dinner first at a proper restaurant.

✓ Convention Strategy: If you're at Austin Convention Center all day, stock your hotel room with Snap Kitchen meals or walk to 2nd Street for sit-down options.

✓ Hotel Room Stocking: Whole Foods flagship store is 10-min walk from downtown. Stock up on GF snacks, breakfast items, and backup meals.

🙋 Frequently Asked Questions

What's the best gluten-free restaurant in downtown Austin?

ATX Cocina on 2nd Street. It's 100% gluten-free, upscale-casual, and perfectly located. Order anything on the menu stress-free.

Are there quick gluten-free lunch options downtown?

Yes! Snap Kitchen (grab-and-go, 2 min), Chipotle (bowls, 10 min), Thundercloud (GF subs, 10 min), and The Well (bowls, 15 min) are all fast and downtown-located.

Is downtown Austin gluten-free friendly?

Very. Downtown has excellent GF options including two 100% gluten-free restaurants (ATX Cocina, The Well, Picnik) and many others with solid GF menus. You won't go hungry.

What's the best gluten-free restaurant for business dinners downtown?

Red Ash for Italian, ATX Cocina for Mexican, or Salt Traders for seafood. All are upscale with excellent GF accommodations. Red Ash and ATX Cocina are most central.

Are there gluten-free options on 6th Street Austin?

Limited. 6th Street is primarily bars with fried bar food. Torchy's Tacos on East 6th has GF corn tortillas. Otherwise, eat dinner before going to 6th Street for nightlife.

Where can I get gluten-free pizza downtown Austin?

Downtown doesn't have dedicated pizza spots with GF crust. Your best options are Via 313 (East 6th, short walk) or Uber to Pinthouse Pizza on South Lamar. See our GF Pizza Guide.
