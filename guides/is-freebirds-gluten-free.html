🌯 Is Freebirds Gluten Free?

Complete guide to gluten-free options at Freebirds World Burrito in Austin

Home > Guides > Is Freebirds Gluten Free?

Quick Answer: Is Freebirds Gluten Free?

⚠️ YES, with Significant Caution

Freebirds World Burrito offers gluten-free options including corn tortillas, bowls, and naturally GF ingredients. However, they use a shared kitchen with high cross-contamination risks. They cannot guarantee items are 100% gluten-free due to shared prep surfaces, cooking equipment, and utensils.

Best for: Gluten-sensitive individuals who can tolerate trace amounts
Risky for: Celiac disease or severe gluten allergies

🌯 Gluten-Free Options at Freebirds

1. Burrito Bowls (Safest Choice)

Skip the flour tortilla and order a bowl - this eliminates the primary gluten source:

  • Corn Tortillas - Available as a gluten-free alternative (shared prep area)
  • Bowls - Build your own with naturally GF ingredients
  • Salads - Fresh greens with GF toppings (skip crispy tortilla strips)

2. Gluten-Free Proteins

All Freebirds proteins are naturally gluten-free:

  • Dark Meat Chicken - Seasoned and grilled
  • Steak - Marinated and grilled
  • Carnitas - Slow-cooked pork
  • Ground Beef - Seasoned beef
  • Vegetarian Beans - Protein-rich option

3. Gluten-Free Bases & Sides

  • Cilantro Lime Rice - Naturally GF
  • Spanish Rice - Naturally GF
  • Black Beans - GF
  • Pinto Beans - GF
  • Refried Beans - GF (verify preparation)

4. Fresh Toppings (Naturally GF)

  • Lettuce, tomatoes, onions, jalapeños
  • Guacamole, sour cream, pico de gallo
  • Cheese (cheddar, Monterey Jack blend)
  • Salsa varieties (verify each time)
  • Corn (not the crispy tortilla strips)

⚠️ Cross-Contamination Risks

High Risk Factors:

  • Shared Prep Surfaces - All ingredients prepared on the same line
  • Shared Cooking Equipment - Grills, pans, and warmers used for all items
  • Flour Tortillas Nearby - Flour dust in the air throughout prep area
  • Shared Utensils - Same serving spoons used across ingredients
  • No Dedicated GF Protocol - Staff don't change gloves or use separate prep areas

⚠️ Celiac Warning: Freebirds does NOT have a dedicated gluten-free kitchen or preparation protocol. Cross-contamination is almost inevitable in their assembly-line format.

🛡️ How to Order Safely at Freebirds

For Gluten-Sensitive (Non-Celiac):

  1. Order a bowl instead of a burrito
  2. Request corn tortillas if you want them on the side
  3. Ask about salsas and sauces - some may contain gluten
  4. Skip crispy toppings - tortilla strips, chips may have cross-contact
  5. Inform the staff about your dietary needs

For Celiac Disease:

Proceed with Extreme Caution

  • Freebirds does NOT have celiac-safe protocols
  • High risk of flour cross-contamination throughout the kitchen
  • Staff are not trained in gluten-free preparation
  • Consider dedicated GF Mexican restaurants instead

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Are Freebirds corn tortillas gluten-free?

Yes, Freebirds corn tortillas are made from corn and are naturally gluten-free. However, they're prepared in the same area as flour tortillas, which creates cross-contamination risks. If you have celiac disease, this is a significant concern.

Does Freebirds have a gluten-free menu?

Freebirds doesn't publish a specific gluten-free menu, but they do provide allergen information through their nutrition calculator online. You can customize bowls and salads with naturally gluten-free ingredients, but they warn that cross-contamination can occur.

Are Freebirds tortilla chips gluten-free?

Freebirds corn tortilla chips are made from corn, which is naturally gluten-free. However, they may be fried in shared oil with items containing gluten, or prepared near flour tortillas. Always ask staff about their current frying practices.

Can I eat at Freebirds with celiac disease?

Freebirds is high-risk for celiac disease due to their shared kitchen environment. While they offer corn tortillas and naturally GF ingredients, the assembly-line format with flour tortillas present creates inevitable cross-contamination. If you have celiac, consider dedicated gluten-free Mexican restaurants in Austin instead.

What should I avoid at Freebirds if I'm gluten-free?

Avoid all flour tortillas, crispy tortilla strips, breaded items, and any sauces that may contain gluten (ask staff to check). Be cautious with any item from shared fryers or cooking surfaces.

Does Freebirds change gloves for gluten-free orders?

Freebirds does not have a standardized gluten-free preparation protocol. You can request fresh gloves, but staff are not specifically trained for celiac-safe preparation. Cross-contamination from shared surfaces and equipment remains a significant concern even with fresh gloves.

💡 Smart Tips for Ordering GF at Freebirds

🥗 Choose Bowls Over Burritos

Bowls eliminate flour tortilla contact entirely and are easier to keep gluten-free.

🗣️ Communicate Clearly

Tell staff you need gluten-free and ask them to change gloves, though this doesn't eliminate all risks.

📱 Check Online First

Use Freebirds' nutrition calculator to verify ingredients before visiting.

⏰ Visit Off-Peak

During slower hours, staff can take more care with preparation and answer questions.

🚫 Skip the Chips

If chips are fried in shared oil, there's a cross-contamination risk.

🌮 Keep It Simple

Stick to obviously GF ingredients: rice, beans, protein, fresh veggies, cheese.

📋 The Bottom Line

Freebirds CAN work for gluten-free diners who:

  • Have mild gluten sensitivity (not celiac)
  • Can tolerate trace cross-contamination
  • Stick to simple bowls with corn tortillas
  • Communicate clearly with staff
  • Visit during off-peak hours

Freebirds is NOT recommended for:

  • Anyone with celiac disease
  • Severe gluten allergies or sensitivity
  • Those who need guaranteed 100% GF preparation
  • Anyone with previous reactions to cross-contamination

When in doubt, choose a dedicated gluten-free Mexican restaurant for peace of mind.
