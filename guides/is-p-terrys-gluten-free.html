🍔 Is P. Terry's Gluten Free?

Everything you need to know about eating gluten-free at P. Terry's Burger Stand

Home > Guides > Is P. Terry's Gluten Free?

📅 Last Updated: March 20, 2026

⚠️ Limited Gluten-Free Options

P. Terry's does NOT offer gluten-free buns. You can order bunless burgers with GF fries, but the experience is underwhelming for celiacs.

P. Terry's Burger Stand—the Austin-based fast food chain known for affordable burgers, shakes, and local ingredients—is a frequent question from the gluten-free community. The reality: P. Terry's is not particularly gluten-free friendly. While you technically CAN eat there with celiac disease, the options are extremely limited and not especially satisfying.

This guide covers what's safe to order, why P. Terry's falls short for GF diners, and much better burger alternatives in Austin.

🍔 What's Gluten-Free at P. Terry's?

✅ Safe to Order

French Fries (Celiac-Safe!)

Good news: P. Terry's fries are gluten-free and cooked in a dedicated fryer with 100% vegetable oil. No breaded items share the fryer, making them safe for celiac disease.

The fries are hand-cut and pretty good—this is P. Terry's best GF offering.

Bunless Burgers (Limited Experience):

  • Single or double burger patties — 100% beef, naturally gluten-free
  • Lettuce, tomato, onion, pickles — all safe toppings
  • Cheese — American cheese is GF
  • Condiments: Ketchup, mustard, mayo are GF

How it's served: You can request a lettuce wrap or have the burger served in a container with a fork. Neither option is particularly satisfying compared to an actual GF bun.

Shakes (if you're having fries only):

  • Vanilla, chocolate, and strawberry shakes appear to be GF
  • Verify with staff that no cookie/brownie mix-ins are added
  • Shared blender risk—ask if they rinse between shakes

❌ NOT Gluten-Free

⚠️ What P. Terry's Doesn't Have

No Gluten-Free Buns: This is the deal-breaker. P. Terry's has no plans to offer GF buns according to their current menu.

Chicken Sandwich: Breaded with wheat flour—not safe.

Regular Buns: Obviously contain gluten.

⚠️ Cross-Contamination Considerations

While P. Terry's fries are cooked in a dedicated fryer (great!), there are other cross-contamination concerns:

1. Shared Grill

Burger patties and regular buns cook on the same flat-top grill. Gluten from buns can transfer to burger surfaces during cooking.

💡 Mitigation: Request your burger be cooked in a clean area of the grill, though this may not always be feasible during busy times.

2. Shared Prep Surfaces

Burgers are assembled on the same surfaces where buns are handled. Flour dust and crumbs can contaminate toppings.

💡 Mitigation: Request fresh gloves and a clean surface if you have celiac.

3. Limited Staff Training

P. Terry's is a fast-food operation with high turnover. Staff training on dietary restrictions varies widely by location and employee.

💡 Mitigation: Be very clear: "I have celiac disease and need a bunless burger with fresh gloves and no contact with buns."

📋 How to Order at P. Terry's with Celiac

If you're determined to eat at P. Terry's, here's the safest approach:

  1. Order at the speaker/counter: "I have celiac disease. I need a burger with NO BUN, lettuce wrap or in a container, with fresh gloves please."
  2. Specify toppings: "Lettuce, tomato, pickles, ketchup, mustard. No bun contact."
  3. Order fries: "And fries—I know they're cooked in a dedicated fryer, which is great."
  4. Watch for understanding: If staff seem confused or dismissive, consider going elsewhere. Clear communication is essential.
  5. Check your order: Verify no bun pieces accidentally got in the container.

🙋 Frequently Asked Questions

Is P. Terry's gluten free?

P. Terry's has very limited gluten-free options. No GF buns are available—only bunless burgers and GF fries. It's technically possible to eat there with celiac, but the experience is poor compared to restaurants that actually accommodate GF diners.

Does P. Terry's have gluten-free buns?

No. P. Terry's does not offer gluten-free buns and has shown no indication they plan to add them. You must order burgers without a bun (lettuce wrap or in a container).

Are P. Terry's fries gluten free?

Yes! P. Terry's fries are gluten-free and cooked in a dedicated fryer (no shared oil with breaded items). This is their only truly celiac-safe menu item besides bunless burgers.

Can you get a lettuce wrap at P. Terry's?

Yes, you can request a lettuce wrap instead of a bun. However, it's not an official menu item—they'll do their best but it's not a polished offering like you'd get at other burger chains.

Is P. Terry's safe for celiac disease?

Marginally. The fries are safe (dedicated fryer), but bunless burgers have cross-contamination risk from the shared grill. Staff training is inconsistent. You can eat there if necessary, but it's not an enjoyable GF experience.

Why doesn't P. Terry's have gluten-free buns?

P. Terry's focuses on a streamlined menu with minimal SKUs (stock-keeping units) to keep prices low and operations simple. Adding GF buns would complicate their model. That's their choice, but it means GF diners are an afterthought.

What should I order at P. Terry's if I'm gluten free?

If you must eat at P. Terry's: Bunless burger (lettuce wrap), fries, and maybe a shake. Specify celiac disease and request fresh gloves. But honestly? Go to Hopdoddy instead.

Are P. Terry's shakes gluten free?

The basic shakes (vanilla, chocolate, strawberry) appear to be gluten-free, but verify they don't add any cookie/brownie mix-ins. Also ask about blender cleaning between shakes to avoid cross-contamination.
