1. Make Reservations & Note GF Needs

When booking, add a note about gluten-free/celiac requirements. This gives the kitchen advance notice and often results in better accommodations.

2. Let Your Date Know (If New)

If it's early in a relationship, a simple "I need to eat gluten-free for health reasons" is enough. Most people are understanding, and a partner who isn't supportive of your health needs is showing you something important.

3. Choose 100% GF for Maximum Romance

When you want zero stress, pick a fully dedicated GF restaurant like ATX Cocina or NÃ´madÃ©. You can focus entirely on each other instead of ingredient lists.

4. Avoid First-Date Kitchen Interrogations
