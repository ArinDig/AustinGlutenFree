üìã Tips for Ordering Gluten-Free Pizza Safely

Whether you're dining at a dedicated GF restaurant or a pizzeria with GF options, these tips will help ensure a safer experience:

Before You Go

  • Call ahead to confirm GF crust availability and ask about their preparation process
  • Check recent reviews from other GF/celiac diners on Find Me Gluten Free or Google
  • Go during off-peak hours when the kitchen is less rushed and can take more care
  • Research the crust brand‚Äîknowing if it's certified GF can give peace of mind

When Ordering

  • Clearly state your needs: "I have celiac disease" or "I need this prepared gluten-free"
  • Ask specific questions: "Is the GF crust made in a separate area?" "Do you use a dedicated pizza screen?"
  • Request fresh gloves and clean surfaces for your pizza preparation
  • Avoid busy periods when cross-contamination risk increases

What to Ask

  1. "Is your gluten-free crust certified GF?"
  2. "Do you have a separate oven or pizza screen for GF orders?"
  3. "Are the toppings stored separately from flour/gluten items?"
  4. "Can you change gloves before preparing my pizza?"
  5. "Is the pizza cut with a clean cutter?"

‚ö†Ô∏è Important for Celiac Diners

For shared-kitchen pizzerias, assess your personal tolerance level and always communicate clearly with staff.

‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

Where can I get the best gluten-free pizza in Austin?

For the safest and most authentic GF pizza experience, Pieous in Dripping Springs is the top choice with their dedicated GF oven. For 100% gluten-free restaurants, try ATX Cocina's tlayudas or The Well's flatbreads. For traditional pizzeria-style, Pinthouse Pizza and Via 313 have well-reviewed GF crusts.

Is gluten-free pizza at regular pizzerias safe for celiacs?

It depends on the restaurant and your sensitivity level. Most Austin pizzerias prepare GF crusts in shared environments, meaning some cross-contamination is likely. For strict celiac safety, stick to dedicated GF restaurants or Pieous's dedicated oven. Those with gluten sensitivity (non-celiac) generally do well at pizzerias that offer GF crusts.

How much extra does GF pizza crust cost in Austin?

Most restaurants charge $2-4 extra for a gluten-free crust. Some upscale establishments may charge more. It's always a good idea to confirm pricing when ordering.

Do any Austin pizzerias have dedicated GF prep areas?

Pieous is the only Austin-area pizzeria we know of with a dedicated gluten-free oven. Most other pizzerias use shared equipment but can take precautions like fresh pizza screens and clean surfaces when requested.

What gluten-free pizza crust brands do Austin restaurants use?

Common brands include Caulipower (cauliflower-based), various certified GF pre-made crusts, and some restaurants make their own. Ask your server if you want to know the specific brand for allergen information.
