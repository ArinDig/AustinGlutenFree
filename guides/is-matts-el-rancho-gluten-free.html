🌮 Is Matt's El Rancho Gluten Free?

Everything you need to know about eating gluten-free at Matt's El Rancho

Home > Guides > Is Matt's El Rancho Gluten Free?

📅 Last Updated: March 20, 2026

⚠️ Limited Options, High Cross-Contamination Risk

Matt's El Rancho has corn tortillas and some GF items, but cross-contamination is a major concern. Proceed with caution if celiac.

Matt's El Rancho—the South Austin Tex-Mex institution since 1952, famous for the Bob Armstrong dip—is beloved by locals. The reality for GF diners: Matt's has limited accommodations. Corn tortillas are available, but the old-school kitchen with shared fryers and griddles makes this risky for celiacs.

🌮 What's Potentially GF at Matt's?

⚠️ Verify everything with staff—recipes and prep methods vary.

  • ✅ Corn tortillas (shared griddle with flour)
  • ✅ Fajitas with corn tortillas (grilled proteins, peppers, onions)
  • ✅ Enchiladas on corn tortillas (verify sauce has no flour)
  • ⚠️ Bob Armstrong Dip (queso may contain flour—ASK FIRST)
  • ❌ Chips (likely fried in shared oil)

💡 Honest Assessment

Matt's El Rancho is NOT celiac-friendly. Old-school Tex-Mex with minimal GF protocols. If you love Matt's, consider ATX Cocina or Chuy's instead for safer Mexican food.
