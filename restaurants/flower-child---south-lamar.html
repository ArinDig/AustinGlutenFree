Gluten-Free Dining at Flower Child

Flower Child is exceptionally GF-friendly, making it one of the easier fast-casual options for celiac diners in Austin:

  • Clearly Marked Menu: GF items are prominently labeled with a "GF" indicator, removing the guesswork
  • Extensive GF Options: The majority of bowls, salads, and protein options are naturally gluten-free
  • Customizable Plates: Build your own plate or bowl by selecting from GF-safe ingredients
  • Knowledgeable Staff: Team members are well-trained on dietary restrictions and can guide you through safe options
  • App & Online Ordering: Filter specifically for GF options when ordering through their app or website
  • Ingredient Transparency: Staff can provide ingredient information upon request

Cross-Contamination Note: While Flower Child has extensive GF options, this is not a dedicated gluten-free kitchen. Shared prep surfaces and equipment are used. Inform staff about celiac disease so they can take extra precautions, such as changing gloves and using clean utensils. Most celiac diners report good experiences, but those with extreme sensitivity should assess their comfort level.

Build Your Own Plate

One of the best features for GF diners is the ability to build your own plate:

  • Choose Your Base: Ancient grains, leafy greens, or combine both
  • Pick Your Protein: Grass-fed steak, organic chicken, wild salmon, organic tofu, or portobello
  • Add Sides: Seasonal vegetables, sweet potato, avocado, and more
  • Select Dressing: Multiple GF-safe dressing options available

This customization ensures you get exactly what you want while staying safely gluten-free.

What to Avoid

Be mindful of these items when ordering gluten-free at Flower Child:

  • Sandwiches and wraps: Contain gluten unless specifically marked GF
  • Some dressings: While most are GF, always confirm with staff
  • Breaded proteins: Any breaded or crusted items
  • Certain desserts: Only the GF Brownie is confirmed safe‚Äîother desserts may contain gluten
  • Some grain options: While quinoa and ancient grains are typically safe, always confirm preparation methods
