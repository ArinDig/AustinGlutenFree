About Casa de Luz

Casa de Luz is far more than a restaurant‚Äîit's a living testament to the power of whole plants, shared meals, and a culture of well-being. Often described as a "temple of connection," Casa de Luz has been serving Austin's health-conscious community since 1991, offering a completely safe haven for those with celiac disease and gluten sensitivities.

Every dish served at Casa de Luz is 100% organic, 100% vegan, and 100% gluten-free. The kitchen does not use processed oils, artificial additives, or GMOs. Instead, they derive essential fatty acids from freshly ground seeds and nuts, creating sauces and dressings that nourish the body while honoring the wholeness of the plants themselves.

Why Casa de Luz is Perfect for Celiacs

This is one of the safest dining experiences in Austin for those with celiac disease:

  • 100% Gluten-Free Facility: No gluten ever enters the kitchen‚Äîzero cross-contamination risk
  • No Processed Ingredients: Whole plant foods only, no hidden gluten sources
  • No Seed Oils: No inflammatory oils that can trigger sensitivities
  • Organic Sourcing: Clean ingredients from trusted suppliers
  • Transparent Kitchen: Open kitchen allows you to see food preparation
  • Community-Focused: Staff understands dietary restrictions intimately

The Dining Experience

Casa de Luz operates on a unique communal dining model:

  • Set Menu: A rotating daily menu featuring a complete macrobiotic meal
  • Communal Tables: Shared seating encourages connection and conversation
  • Whole Plant Focus: Grains, vegetables, legumes, and sea vegetables
  • Fresh Daily: Ingredients prepared fresh each day
  • Mindful Eating: A peaceful environment designed for conscious nourishment

Typical meals include a grain (often brown rice), soup, seasonal vegetables, beans or legumes, pickled vegetables, and a sauce or dressing made from ground seeds and nuts.

The Casa de Luz Philosophy

Founded on macrobiotic principles, Casa de Luz believes that food is medicine and community is the healing force. The mission extends beyond the restaurant through the Casa de Luz Foundation, which supports wellness villages across the country built on the same principles of whole plant nutrition, open kitchens, and shared meals.

For those seeking a return to simple, whole foods in a supportive community environment, Casa de Luz offers something truly unique in Austin's dining landscape.
