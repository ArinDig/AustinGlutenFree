About Ka-Prow

Ka-Prow (named after the popular Thai basil stir-fry dish "Pad Kra Pao") is a pan-Asian eatery with a focus on authentic Thai cuisine. The restaurant offers an intimate setting where bold flavors and fresh ingredients come together in dishes that honor Thai culinary traditions.

The name "Ka-Prow" refers to Thai holy basil, the aromatic herb essential to the classic Pad Kra Pao dish. This South Austin gem has built a loyal following among Thai food enthusiasts who appreciate authentic flavors and generous portions.

For gluten-free diners, Thai cuisine offers many naturally GF options built around rice, vegetables, and coconut‚Äîand Ka-Prow is willing to accommodate dietary needs with proper communication.

Gluten-Free Dining at Ka-Prow

Ka-Prow offers several GF-friendly options with some important considerations:

  • Rice-Based Dishes: All rice dishes‚Äîjasmine, sticky, and fried rice‚Äîstart with naturally GF rice
  • Curries: Thai curry pastes are traditionally GF; the coconut milk base is also safe
  • Stir-Fries: Can be made with GF soy sauce (tamari) upon request
  • Fresh Ingredients: Focus on whole, fresh ingredients typical of Thai cooking
  • Accommodating Staff: Team is willing to work with dietary restrictions

Critical Note: Traditional soy sauce contains wheat. Always request GF soy sauce (tamari) as a substitution, and clearly inform your server about celiac disease or gluten allergy. Communication is key at this restaurant.

Recommended GF Menu Items

  • Green Curry: Coconut curry with bamboo, Thai basil, and your choice of protein
  • Red Curry: Rich, aromatic curry with bell peppers and vegetables
  • Massaman Curry: Milder curry with potatoes, peanuts, and warm spices
  • Pad Thai: Rice noodles with tamarind sauce‚Äîconfirm all ingredients are GF
  • Basil Stir-Fry (Ka-Prow): The namesake dish with holy basil, chilies, and garlic
  • Tom Yum Soup: Hot and sour soup with lemongrass and galangal
  • Tom Kha: Creamy coconut soup with similar aromatics
  • Jasmine Rice: Plain steamed rice is always a safe side

What to Avoid or Confirm

  • Regular soy sauce: Contains wheat‚Äîalways request tamari/GF substitution
  • Oyster sauce: Some brands contain wheat‚Äîconfirm with kitchen
  • Fried items: May be breaded or cooked in shared fryer oil
  • Spring rolls: Wrappers typically contain wheat flour
  • Wontons/dumplings: Wheat-based wrappers
  • Some noodle dishes: Confirm noodles are rice-based, not wheat

Tips for Ordering at Ka-Prow

Maximize your chances of a safe, delicious meal:

  • Call ahead: Ask if they have tamari/GF soy sauce on hand
  • Be specific: Say "celiac disease" or "gluten allergy"‚Äînot just "gluten-free preference"
  • Request clearly: "Please make this with GF soy sauce only"
  • Stick to curries: Curries are often the safest bet‚Äîthey're coconut-based with minimal sauce additions
  • Confirm rice noodles: Make sure noodles are 100% rice
  • Go off-peak: Kitchen has more time to accommodate requests during slower periods
