About Hopdoddy Burger Bar

Hopdoddy Burger Bar is an Austin institution that helped define the craft burger movement when it opened on South Congress in 2010. Known for their fresh-ground-daily beef, creative burger combinations, hand-cut Kennebec fries, adult milkshakes, and craft cocktails, Hopdoddy set a new standard for what a burger restaurant could be.

The South Congress location is the original Hopdoddy‚Äîthe one that started it all‚Äîand its legendary lines out the door have become as iconic as the street itself. There's something special about standing in that queue on a beautiful Austin day, watching the eclectic SoCo crowd pass by while anticipating one of the city's best burgers.

For gluten-free diners, Hopdoddy offers quality GF bun substitutions, making their exceptional burgers accessible to the celiac community. While this isn't a dedicated GF kitchen, their commitment to offering alternatives means GF burger lovers don't have to miss out on the Hopdoddy experience.

Gluten-Free Dining at Hopdoddy

Hopdoddy provides several options for gluten-free guests to enjoy their menu:

  • GF Buns Available: Substitute a quality gluten-free bun on any burger for a small upcharge
  • Lettuce Wrap Option: Get any burger wrapped in crisp lettuce‚Äînaturally GF and refreshingly light
  • 100% Fresh Beef: All beef patties are naturally gluten-free, ground fresh daily
  • Salad Options: Burger salad versions available‚Äîyour choice of patty over greens
  • Shakes: Many shake flavors are naturally GF (always confirm with staff about mix-ins)
  • Sides: Check which sides are GF-safe‚Äîsome may be cooked in shared fryers

Cross-Contamination Note: Hopdoddy uses shared grills for all burgers, and fries are cooked in shared fryers. While GF buns are available, those with severe celiac or extreme sensitivity should be aware of potential cross-contamination. Always inform your server about celiac disease so they can take extra precautions.

Tips for a Great GF Hopdoddy Visit

  • Off-Peak Hours: Visit during less busy times (mid-afternoon) for faster service and potentially more attention to GF preparation
  • Communicate Clearly: Tell your server about celiac disease, not just GF preference‚Äîthis triggers more careful handling
  • Skip the Fries: If you're highly sensitive, consider skipping fries due to shared fryer concerns
  • Lettuce Wrap: For maximum safety, consider the lettuce wrap option‚Äîno bun contamination concerns
